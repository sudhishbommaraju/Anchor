It catches the loop, then does the work.

A repeated step (cosine ≥ 0.85) is a loop; an off-goal step (≤ 0.15 to the goal) is drift. Anchor embeds the step, queries pgvector, and injects a corrective — before the model ever sees it.

  • Thresholds calibrated empirically, not guessed
  • Auto-correct, or alert-only — your call per mission

What happens on every call.

One request in, one response out — and six things in between, every single time.

01

Authenticate the key

The anc_live_ key (sha-256 hash) resolves to your mission.

02

Embed the step

The latest action is embedded with text-embedding-3-small.

03

Detect loop & drift

One pgvector query vs recent steps and the goal.

04

Inject system block

Mission + live memory + a corrective if something was caught.

05

Forward to model

OpenRouter or your BYOK key. Streaming passes through.

06

Update & log

Store the step + embedding, dedupe memory, record spend.
